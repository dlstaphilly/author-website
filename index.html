David Lydon-Staley

Fiction Writer & Assistant Professor of Communication

David Lydon-Staley is a fiction writer. His work explores queer identity, intimacy, and the quiet moments that shape relationships. Often grounded in place, especially Ireland and Philadelphia, his fiction blends humor and heartache, and features characters navigating the space between connection and solitude. He is represented by Jim McCarthy at Dystel, Goderich & Bourret, LLC

His short stories appear in Cleaver Magazine and Jonathan: A Queer Fiction Journal. You can find him talking about books he loves and the Grand Duchess Anastasia (AKA Stacey the Pug) on TikTok @dlstaphilly.

Originally from Galway, Ireland, David lives in Philadelphia, where he is pursuing an M.F.A. in Creative Writing at Drexel University and is also an Assistant Professor of Communication at the University of Pennsylvania. His academic research explores curiosity, emotion dynamics, and substance use in daily life, with work appearing in Nature Human Behavior, Science Advances, and Nature Neuroscience.
